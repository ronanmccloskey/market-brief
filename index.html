S&P 500 & Nasdaq Surge to Record Closes; Apple Powers Tech Rally
›
Equities

The S&P 500 rose 0.29% to 7,230.12 and the Nasdaq Composite climbed 0.89% to 25,114.44 on Friday, with both indexes posting fresh all-time closing highs. The DJI slipped 0.31% to 49,499.27 as cyclical and value names lagged the tech-led advance.

AAPL gained more than 3% after beating fiscal Q2 earnings and revenue estimates, with management citing "extraordinary" demand for the iPhone 17 lineup and issuing better-than-expected current-quarter revenue guidance. Software names FIVN (+30%), TEAM, and TWLO surged on strong results, reinforcing confidence in enterprise tech spending.

The weekly scorecard: S&P +0.91%, Nasdaq +1.12%, Dow +0.55%. April was the S&P 500's best month since November 2020, gaining over 10% even as oil tops $100 and bond yields crept higher.

Knock-on Effects

Rotation risk: The Dow's underperformance reflects narrow breadth — mega-cap tech masks weakness in industrials and financials.
Earnings season momentum: With FactSet projecting 13.2% S&P 500 Q1 earnings growth (sixth consecutive quarter of double-digit growth), investors will test that thesis against AMD, Disney, and PayPal this week.
Berkshire signal: Greg Abel's first annual meeting revealed a record $397.4B cash pile and 14th consecutive quarter of net stock sales — a cautious read on valuations.

Trump Threatens 25% EU Auto Tariffs, Rattling Trade Truce
›
Macro Equities

President Trump announced Friday he will raise tariffs on cars and trucks from the EU to 25% starting next week, accusing Brussels of failing to comply with the 2025 Turnberry Agreement that had set a 15% ceiling on most goods. The White House said the hike will be implemented via Section 232, the national security tariff authority that has survived court challenges.

VLKAF, MBGAF, and BMWYY are the European automakers most exposed, shipping a large share of US sales from European plants. Shares of US automakers F (-2%), STLA (-1.7%), and GM (-1.5%) also slid on the news. The EU Commission rejected accusations of non-compliance and said it would "keep options open to protect EU interests."

Knock-on Effects
Sticker-price inflation: 25% tariffs could push up consumer prices on European vehicles at US dealerships, adding to already-elevated inflation running at 3.3% annually.
EU retaliation risk: Brussels had been legislating the tariff deal; a trade breakdown could trigger counter-measures on US goods.
Production shifts: The carve-out for EU cars built in US plants may accelerate European OEMs' reshoring plans, benefiting southern US manufacturing hubs.
EUR/USD watch: The euro held at 1.1723 (+0.33%) for now, but a full-blown trade war would pressure the single currency.
Source: 1440, Bloomberg, CNBC

Pentagon Signs AI Deals with 8 Tech Giants — Anthropic Left Out
›
Equities Macro

The Department of Defense announced agreements with eight major tech firms — GOOG, NVDA, MSFT, AMZN, ORCL, OpenAI, SpaceX, and Reflection AI — to deploy AI on classified military networks at Impact Level 6 and 7. Pentagon CTO Emil Michael called it a step toward making the military an "AI-first fighting force."

Notably absent is Anthropic, whose Claude model had been the only AI deployed on classified networks until a bitter dispute over usage terms. The Pentagon branded Anthropic a "supply chain risk" after the company refused to allow use for autonomous weapons and domestic mass surveillance — a designation a federal judge later blocked. The new deals give the Pentagon vendor diversification across proprietary and open-source models, with Nvidia's Nemotron agents a highlight.

Knock-on Effects
Defense revenue boost: The $961.6B defense budget request, with $33.7B for science and autonomous systems, creates a growing addressable market for AI vendors.
Anthropic competitive pressure: Losing classified revenue while 8 rivals gain access weakens Anthropic's enterprise positioning and may force concessions.
Open-source momentum: Nvidia and Reflection AI's inclusion signals the Pentagon's embrace of inspectable, modifiable models for national security use cases.
GenAI.mil scale: Over 1.3 million DOD personnel have used the platform to deploy hundreds of thousands of AI agents since December.
