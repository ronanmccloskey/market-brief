›
Equities

The SPX rose 0.29% to 7,230.12 and the COMP climbed 0.89% to 25,114.44, both posting record closes on Friday. The DJI slipped 0.31% to 49,499.27 as value and cyclicals lagged while mega-cap tech led.

CEO Tim Cook cited "extraordinary demand" for the iPhone 17 lineup. Services revenue hit an all-time high of $31 billion, and gross margin expanded to 49.3%. Apple also guided for 14%–17% revenue growth next quarter versus the 9.5% Street expectation.

The results cap a month where the S&P 500 and Nasdaq posted their strongest monthly gains since 2020, powered by robust earnings: over 80% of S&P 500 reporters have beaten expectations so far, with blended earnings growth of 32% — the highest since Q4 2021.

Trump Threatens to Hike EU Auto Tariffs to 25%, Reigniting Trade Tensions
›
Macro Equities

President Trump announced Friday he will raise tariffs on EU cars and trucks from 15% to 25% as early as this week, accusing the bloc of failing to comply with the Turnberry Agreement negotiated last July. The White House said the increase will run through Section 232 national security authority, the one tariff tool that has survived Supreme Court challenge.

European automakers VWAGY, MBGAF, and BMWYY are the most exposed since they ship a large share of U.S. sales from European plants. Domestic names also felt pressure: F fell 2%, STLA dropped 1.7%, and GM slid 1.5% following the announcement. The EU rejected the compliance accusation and said it would "keep our options open to protect EU interests."

Knock-on Effects
Price impact: A 25% rate could push up sticker prices at U.S. dealers or compress European automakers' margins
Production shift: Trump's exemption for EU cars built in the U.S. may accelerate reshoring decisions by German OEMs
USMCA watch: Automakers are privately delaying production shifts until July's USMCA formal review provides clarity
Escalation risk: The EU warned of retaliatory measures, adding another dimension to the already fraught global trade environment
